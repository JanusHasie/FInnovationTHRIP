Innovation Capability

Innovation capability indicates the level to which an organisation supports systemic innovation. In other words, how EASY or DIFFICULT is it for staff members to originate and implement strategically aligned ideas in the organisation. It’s based on the organisations ability to convert its intellectual capital quickly into new products, services or business value. The Innovation Capability of an organisation is usually indicated by an Innovation Capability Index, which is expressed as a percentage (%).

Your organisation’s ideal state is one where you are potentially leveraging all the latent human capital available to the organisation to solve different challenges the organisation are faced with. In this ideal state the organisation is using all the idea power of everybody in the ecosystem to its challenges. In this ideal state all tacit and explicit knowledge is transformed into business value and opportunities for future growth. Creating business value could be seen as the ability to convert intellectual capital into new products and services or other related business value.

Innovation refers to the ability a organisation has to convert intellectual capital quickly into new products or services, In other words how quickly the company can turn knowledge into business value through the necessary organisational support. The effectiveness of any innovation management programme is linked to the level of organisational enablement and is referred to at the innovation capability.

The innovation capability is an indicator of how easy or difficult it is for employees to submit and implement strategically aligned ideas.
